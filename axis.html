Axis Communications — Master Thesis  |  01/2026 – Present  |  Lund, Sweden

Streaming Dynamic 3D Scene Reconstruction from Sparse Multi-Camera Video

Overview

Dynamic scene reconstruction with a focus on near real-time training, rendering, and end-to-end streaming pipeline development. Investigating methods to reduce training time and camera count while maintaining high reconstruction quality.

Contributions

  1. Multi-camera data acquisition system.

    Developed a data acquisition system capable of synchronizing up to 40 cameras. The system allows users to configure camera settings and capture synchronized images or record synchronized video sequences.

  2. Three streaming reconstruction pipelines.

    Evaluated multiple reconstruction methods and implemented three streaming pipelines:

    • VGGT
    • VGGT-initialized FastGS
    • 4DGS

    For the VGGT and VGGT-initialized FastGS pipelines, a segmentation module was implemented and evaluated to enable reconstruction of only the moving foreground objects. All three pipelines were extensively tested and optimized to reduce processing time and improve overall performance.

  3. All-in-one user interface.

    Integrated the complete streaming workflow into a unified user interface. Users can configure pipeline settings, adjust parameters, run different reconstruction methods, and compare their results within the same application.

  4. Improved stability and deployability.

    Refined the overall pipeline to improve stability, usability, and ease of deployment across new multi-camera setups.
